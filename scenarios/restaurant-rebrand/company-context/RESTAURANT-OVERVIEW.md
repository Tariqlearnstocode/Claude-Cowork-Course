## History

Maggie Kowalski opened State Fair Cafe in 1999 after 15 years working at other Detroit diners. She named it after the Michigan State Fair, which her father took her to every year as a child. The restaurant quickly became a Corktown institution.

Key milestones:
- **1999:** Grand opening, 32 seats
- **2004:** Expanded to current 62-seat capacity
- **2008:** Survived economic crash (barely)
- **2012:** Featured on local TV segment "Detroit's Best Breakfasts"
- **2015:** Maggie's husband passes; she considers selling
- **2017:** Corktown gentrification begins; new residents arrive
- **2022:** Revenue decline becomes noticeable
- **2024:** Derek hired to modernize; leaves after 8 months
- **2025:** You arrive

## Typical Customer Profile (Historical)

**The Regulars (estimated 40% of revenue)**
- Age: 55-75
- Frequency: 2-5x per week
- Order: "The usual"
- Been coming for 10+ years
- Know staff by name

**The Weekenders (estimated 35% of revenue)**
- Age: 35-55
- Frequency: Weekend brunch, occasional weekday
- Families with kids
- Looking for reliable, affordable breakfast

**The Workers (estimated 25% of revenue)**
- Age: 25-45
- Frequency: Weekday breakfast/lunch
- Construction crews, nearby office workers
- Fast, filling, fairly priced

## Recent Changes in Customer Base

Since Corktown gentrification accelerated (2019-present):
- New residents: younger, higher income, different expectations
- Some have tried State Fair Cafe, many haven't returned
- Online reviews from new residents often mention "dated" or "nothing special"
- Long-time regulars remain loyal but are literally aging out

## Competitive Position

State Fair Cafe sits in an increasingly competitive landscape:
- Multiple new brunch spots have opened in Corktown (2019-2024)
- Fast-casual chains expanding nearby
- Classic diners in the region are struggling or closing
- Successful survivors have either modernized or doubled down on authenticity
