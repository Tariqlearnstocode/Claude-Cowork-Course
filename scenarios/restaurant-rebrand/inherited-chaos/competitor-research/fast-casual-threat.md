# Analysis: The Fast Casual Threat

*Why we're losing weekday lunch (and some breakfast)*

---

## The Data

State Fair Cafe revenue breakdown by daypart (from Maggie's records):

| Daypart | 2022 | 2023 | 2024 | Change |
|---------|------|------|------|--------|
| Weekday breakfast | $285k | $261k | $214k | -25% |
| Weekday lunch | $156k | $133k | $109k | -30% |
| Weekend brunch | $280k | $288k | $295k | +5% |
| **Total** | **$721k** | **$682k** | **$618k** | **-14%** |

Weekend brunch is actually UP. Our problem is weekday traffic.

---

## What's Happening

### Weekday breakfast decline:
- Work-from-home shift (COVID legacy) - fewer commuters
- Coffee culture shift - people grab coffee at specialty shops, not diners
- Changing habits - younger workers eat at desk, skip sit-down breakfast
- Competition from quick options (Starbucks, Dunkin', fast casual)

### Weekday lunch collapse (-30%!):
- Fast casual explosion in Corktown/surrounding area
- Time pressure - office workers want faster service
- Health consciousness - salads/bowls vs. diner food
- Ordering apps - easy to get delivery from anywhere

---

## Fast Casual Competitors (within 1.5 miles)

### First Watch
**Type:** Breakfast/brunch chain
**Opened nearby:** 2023
**Avg check:** $15-18
**Wait time:** Minimal (efficient operations)

**Strengths:**
- Clean, bright, modern
- Consistent quality
- Health-forward options (egg white, fresh juice, salads)
- Technology (app ordering, loyalty program)
- Large menu with something for everyone

**Weaknesses:**
- No soul, no story
- Same as every other First Watch
- No regulars in the community sense

**Impact on State Fair Cafe:**
Direct competition for weekday breakfast. They're faster, have healthier options, and feel more "current." Hard to compete head-to-head.

---

### Sweetgreen
**Type:** Salad/grain bowl chain
**Opened nearby:** 2022
**Avg check:** $14-17

**Impact:**
Taking our health-conscious lunch customers. We can't compete with their menu (nor should we try). Different category.

---

### Chipotle
**Type:** Fast casual Mexican
**Opened nearby:** 2019 (expanded 2022)
**Avg check:** $12-15

**Impact:**
Fast, customizable, filling, affordable. Taking our lunch crowd who just want a quick meal.

---

### Panera Bread
**Type:** Fast casual bakery-cafe
**Multiple locations nearby**
**Avg check:** $13-18

**Impact:**
Coffee + pastry for breakfast. Soup + sandwich for lunch. Fast, clean, predictable. They've been chipping away at traditional diners for 20 years.

---

## What Fast Casual Gets Right

1. **Speed** - Order to food in <10 minutes
2. **Consistency** - Same experience every time
3. **Technology** - App ordering, loyalty rewards, easy payment
4. **Customization** - Build your own options
5. **Health perception** - Even if not actually healthier, they FEEL healthier
6. **Clean aesthetics** - Modern, bright, well-maintained

## What Fast Casual Gets Wrong

1. **No soul** - Interchangeable, forgettable
2. **No community** - No regulars, no relationships
3. **No surprise** - No discovery, no delight
4. **No authenticity** - Corporate, formulaic

---

## Can State Fair Cafe Compete?

**Head-to-head on speed/convenience: NO**
We can't out-fast-casual the fast casual chains. They have systems, scale, and investment we don't.

**Head-to-head on health: NO**
We're a diner. Our food is eggs and bacon and hash browns. We can add some healthier options but we'll never be Sweetgreen.

**On price: MAYBE**
We're competitive on price. $14.50 average check vs. their $15-17. But that's not a differentiator.

**On experience: YES**
This is where we can win. Fast casual is convenient but soulless. We have:
- History and story
- Real relationships (servers who know your name)
- Authenticity (house-made, real cooking)
- Atmosphere (yes, it's dated, but it's REAL)
- Surprise and delight (Maggie's pies, the jukebox, the regulars at the counter)

---

## Strategic Implications

### Accept what we've lost:
The weekday quick-service customer is probably gone. They want speed; we can't provide it. Fighting for them is a losing battle.

### Defend what we have:
Weekend brunch is strong. Regulars are loyal. Let's not screw that up by chasing trends.

### Carve out a position:
"We're not fast food. We're not fast casual. We're a DINER. That means something."

- Slower? Yes. Worth it? Also yes.
- Less healthy? Sometimes. But made with care.
- Dated? It's called character.

### Add convenience WITHOUT sacrificing identity:
- Online ordering (for pickup, not for speed - for convenience)
- Modern payment options
- Better website and social presence
- Mobile-friendly

The goal isn't to become fast casual. The goal is to make it easier for people who WANT a diner to CHOOSE us.

---

## What We Should NOT Do

1. ~~Add kale salads and grain bowls to compete with Sweetgreen~~
2. ~~Speed up service at the expense of experience~~
3. ~~Gut the interior to look "modern"~~
4. ~~Rebrand to seem "healthy" when we're not~~

These moves would abandon our strengths while failing to match the competition. Worst of both worlds.

---

## What We SHOULD Do

1. Own our identity: "We're a classic diner. That's special now."
2. Make it easy to find us and order from us (technology)
3. Give people a reason to slow down (experience)
4. Win on weekends (double down on brunch strength)
5. Accept weekday decline but stabilize it (don't abandon, but don't over-invest)

---

## Final Thought

Fast casual exists because people want convenience. We can't beat convenience.

But fast casual is also exhausting. Same thing every day. No surprise. No warmth.

Some people - more than you'd think - want something different. They want to be KNOWN. They want to feel like they BELONG.

That's what State Fair Cafe offers. That's our value proposition. Let's not throw it away chasing efficiency.
