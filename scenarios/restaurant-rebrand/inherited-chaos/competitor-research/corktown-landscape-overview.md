# Corktown Restaurant Landscape Overview

*Understanding our neighborhood context*

---

## Corktown: The Transformation

Corktown is Detroit's oldest neighborhood, and it's changed dramatically in the past decade:

**2010-2015:** Early gentrification
- Artists, musicians, urban pioneers
- Cheap rent, gritty character
- A few pioneers opening restaurants (Slows Bar BQ, etc.)

**2015-2020:** Acceleration
- Ford buys Michigan Central Station (massive investment)
- Tech workers and young professionals arrive
- Rent rises, new construction begins
- Restaurant/bar scene explodes

**2020-2025:** Current state
- Full gentrification underway
- Mix of old-timers and newcomers
- Tension between "authentic" Corktown and "new" Corktown
- Restaurant scene is competitive and saturated

## Customer Segmentation in Corktown

Based on observation and research:

### Segment 1: The Old Guard (shrinking)
- Long-time residents, 55+
- Lower income, fixed income
- Nostalgic for old Detroit
- Where they eat: State Fair Cafe, Elwood, neighborhood bars
- What they want: Affordable, familiar, community

### Segment 2: The Newcomers (growing)
- Recent arrivals, 25-40
- Higher income, professionals
- Excited about "discovery," authenticity hunting
- Where they eat: Folk, Gold Cash Gold, Slows
- What they want: Quality, Instagram-worthy, experiences

### Segment 3: The Families (stable)
- Young families with kids
- Mix of new and established
- Looking for affordable, reliable, kid-friendly
- Where they eat: Varies widely, price-sensitive
- What they want: Value, convenience, no judgment

### Segment 4: The Ford Campus (new)
- Tech workers at Ford's Michigan Central
- 25-45, higher income
- Lunch + post-work crowds
- Where they eat: Whatever's fast, convenient, or social
- What they want: Options, speed, after-work scene

## Opportunity Analysis

### Where State Fair Cafe currently wins:
- Segment 1 (Old Guard) - We OWN this segment
- Segment 3 (Families) - Weekend brunch is strong

### Where we're losing:
- Segment 2 (Newcomers) - They try us once and don't return
- Segment 4 (Ford Campus) - We're not on their radar

### The opportunity:
**Can we capture some of Segment 2 WITHOUT losing Segment 1?**

The challenge: These segments want seemingly opposite things:
- Segment 1: Don't change
- Segment 2: Change everything

But is that actually true? Let's look closer:

**What Segment 2 actually wants:**
- Authenticity (we have it)
- Story (we have it)
- Quality (we have it, undersold)
- Modern convenience (we don't have it)
- Instagram-worthy (we could have it)

**What Segment 2 doesn't want:**
- Fake authenticity
- Pretension
- Overpricing
- Attitude

Interesting. Maybe the gap is smaller than it seems.

---

## Competitive Positioning Options

### Option A: Stay the course
- Accept decline, serve existing customers until the end
- Low investment, graceful decline
- Risk: Closes within 5 years

### Option B: Full modernization
- Rebrand, renovate, new menu
- High investment (~$150k+), high risk
- Could work, but competing against established players
- Risk: Lose soul, still fail

### Option C: Authentic evolution
- Stay who we are, but level up
- Medium investment (~$30-50k)
- Add convenience (online ordering, payment)
- Enhance strengths (coffee, pie, atmosphere)
- Tell our story better (social media, PR)
- Risk: Not enough change? Or just right?

---

## My Recommendation (preliminary)

**Option C: Authentic Evolution**

State Fair Cafe has something the trendy spots don't: genuine history and community. That's increasingly rare and valuable.

But we're making it hard for people to find us and choose us. Fix that, and we might be surprised.

Specific moves:
1. Modern website with online ordering
2. Active social media showcasing authenticity (not pretending to be trendy)
3. Payment technology (Apple Pay, tap to pay)
4. Coffee upgrade (quality, not just trendiness)
5. Promote the pie
6. Lean into the story: "25 years in Corktown. Classic Detroit dining."

What we should NOT do:
- Chase trends
- Pretend to be something we're not
- Alienate the regulars

The goal is to make State Fair Cafe easier to discover and choose, while remaining exactly who we are.
