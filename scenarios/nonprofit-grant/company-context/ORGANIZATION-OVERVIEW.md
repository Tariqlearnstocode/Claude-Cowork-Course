## Programs

### After-School Arts Academy
**Schedule:** September through May, Monday-Thursday, 3:30-6:00 PM
**Ages:** Grades 3-12 (approximately ages 8-18)
**Cost:** Free
**Capacity:** ~40 students per day across all classes

**What's Included:**
- Transportation from partner schools (3 bus routes)
- Healthy snack
- All art supplies
- End-of-semester exhibitions

**Disciplines Offered:**
- Visual Arts (painting, drawing, mixed media)
- Ceramics
- Music Production
- Creative Writing
- Theater Arts
- Photography (new this year)

### Summer Arts Intensive
**Schedule:** June through mid-August (6 weeks)
**Hours:** Monday-Friday, 9:00 AM - 3:00 PM
**Ages:** Grades 4-10 (approximately ages 9-16)
**Cost:** Free
**Capacity:** ~60 students per session

**What's Included:**
- Breakfast and lunch (partnership with Gleaners)
- All art supplies
- Field trips to Detroit Institute of Arts, local galleries
- Final exhibition/performance at end of program

*This is the program funded by the Michigan Arts Foundation grant.*

### Saturday Studios
**Schedule:** Year-round, Saturdays 10:00 AM - 2:00 PM
**Ages:** All ages, families welcome
**Cost:** Free
**Format:** Drop-in, no registration required

**Purpose:**
- Community building
- Low-barrier entry point to DYA
- Family engagement
- Alumni connection

### Teen Leadership Program
**Schedule:** Year-round, by application
**Ages:** Grades 9-12
**Capacity:** 15 students per year

**What's Included:**
- Leadership training
- Mentorship of younger students
- College prep and portfolio development
- Stipend for teaching assistant work ($12/hour)
- Alumni network support

## Populations Served

### Geographic Focus
- Primary: Detroit's east side (48207, 48213, 48214, 48215 zip codes)
- Secondary: Other Detroit neighborhoods as transportation allows

### Demographics (FY25 data)
- **Race/Ethnicity:** 78% Black/African American, 12% Hispanic/Latino, 6% Multiracial, 4% Other
- **Gender:** 54% female, 44% male, 2% non-binary/other
- **Income:** 89% qualify for free/reduced lunch at school
- **Special needs:** 14% have IEPs or 504 plans

### Partner Schools
- Clippert Academy (K-8)
- Southeastern High School
- Detroit International Academy (K-8)
- Osborn High School
- FLICS (Foreign Language Immersion and Cultural Studies)

## Facilities

### Main Location: 2847 Gratiot Avenue
- 8,500 sq ft converted warehouse
- Donated by Raymond Foster (board member) - rent-free use through 2030
- Utilities paid by DYA (~$18,000/year)

**Includes:**
- Large open studio space (can accommodate 50+ students)
- Ceramics room with 2 kilns
- Music production suite (4 workstations)
- Small black box theater space
- Kitchen/snack area
- Staff offices
- Storage for supplies

### Transportation
- Partnership with SMART (Suburban Mobility Authority for Regional Transportation)
- 3 bus routes serving partner schools
- Cost: ~$35,000/year
