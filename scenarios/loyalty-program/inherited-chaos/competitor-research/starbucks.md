# Competitor Research: Applebee's

## Program Overview
**Name:** Applebee's Rewards
**Members:** 20+ million active US members
**Structure:** Points-based system

## How It Works
- 1 Point per $1 spent
- 150 Points: Free appetizer
- 250 Points: Free entree
- Birthday reward: Free dessert
- Welcome bonus: Free appetizer on sign-up

## What They Do Well

### 1. Gamification
- "Bonus Point" challenges (earn X points in Y days)
- Happy hour specials for members
- Games and sweepstakes
- Progress bars showing how close you are

### 2. Personalization
- Remembers your favorite orders
- Personalized offers based on dining history
- Birthday reward
- "Just for you" recommendations

### 3. Convenience
- Mobile order ahead
- Easy in-app checkout
- Reload and earn in one step
- Find nearby locations

### 4. Status
- Tier status feels meaningful
- Exclusive early access to new menu items
- Members feel like insiders

## Their Weaknesses
- Can feel corporate and impersonal
- Some customers feel "nickel and dimed"
- The app is complicated
- Less community feel than local spots

## What We Can Learn
- Gamification works (people love progress bars and challenges)
- Personalization matters (knowing your order = feeling valued)
- Make status feel meaningful, not arbitrary
- Convenience is a feature, not just rewards

## Key Stats
- 20M active members
- Responsible for 40%+ of transactions
- Members spend 2-3x more than non-members
- 90-day retention: ~55%

## The Big Takeaway
Applebee's proves that loyalty programs CAN drive behavior - but they do it through personalization, gamification, and convenience, not just points. They make you feel like an insider.

We can't out-Applebee's Applebee's. But we can out-personality them.
