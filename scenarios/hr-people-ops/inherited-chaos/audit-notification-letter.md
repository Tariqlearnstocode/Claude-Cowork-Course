The Michigan Department of Labor and Economic Opportunity (LEO) has selected Woodward Ventures, Inc. for a routine compliance audit. This audit is part of our ongoing efforts to ensure workplace compliance with state and federal employment regulations.

### AUDIT DATE AND TIME

**Date:** February 17, 2026
**Time:** 9:00 AM
**Location:** Your place of business (address above) or virtual option available upon request

### SCOPE OF AUDIT

This audit will review your compliance with the following:

**1. Employment Eligibility Verification (I-9)**
- Form I-9 for all current employees
- Supporting documentation
- Proper completion and retention procedures

**2. Wage and Hour Compliance**
- Minimum wage compliance
- Overtime pay calculations
- Employee classification (exempt vs. non-exempt)
- Pay stub requirements
- Time and attendance records for non-exempt employees

**3. Workplace Safety Compliance**
- Required workplace postings
- Safety training records
- MIOSHA compliance (as applicable)
- Workers' compensation coverage verification

### DOCUMENTS TO PREPARE

Please have the following documents available for review:

**Employment Eligibility:**
- Form I-9 for all current employees
- List of all current employees (name, hire date, position)
- Any I-9 supporting documentation you retain

**Wage and Hour:**
- Payroll records for the past 12 months
- Time and attendance records for all non-exempt employees
- Job descriptions for all positions
- Documentation supporting exempt classification decisions
- Current employee handbook or policy manual

**Workplace Safety:**
- Workers' compensation insurance certificate
- Safety training records
- OSHA 300 log (if applicable)
- Evidence of required workplace postings

### AUDIT PROCESS

1. **Opening Conference:** Brief meeting to discuss audit scope and process
2. **Document Review:** Examination of requested records
3. **Employee Interviews:** We may request brief interviews with selected employees
4. **Site Walk-Through:** Brief inspection of workspace and postings
5. **Closing Conference:** Preliminary findings and next steps

The audit is expected to take 4-6 hours.

### YOUR RIGHTS AND RESPONSIBILITIES

You have the right to:
- Request a postponement for good cause (must be submitted in writing within 10 days)
- Have legal counsel present during the audit
- Receive a copy of all findings
- Appeal any adverse findings through the administrative process

You are responsible for:
- Providing requested documents in an organized manner
- Making appropriate personnel available for questions
- Providing access to workspaces for inspection
- Cooperating with audit staff
