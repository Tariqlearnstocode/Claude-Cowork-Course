### 1. CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

**1.1 Definition.** "Confidential Information" means any and all information or data that has or could have commercial value or other utility in the business in which Company is engaged, including but not limited to:
- Technical data, trade secrets, know-how, and research
- Product plans, software code, and architecture
- Customer lists, sales data, and marketing strategies
- Financial information, business plans, and projections
- Personnel information and organizational structure
- Any other information designated as confidential by Company

**1.2 Non-Disclosure.** Employee agrees to hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence and not to disclose any Confidential Information to any third party without the prior written consent of Company.

**1.3 Non-Use.** Employee agrees not to use any Confidential Information for any purpose other than performing duties for Company.

**1.4 Return of Materials.** Upon termination of employment or upon Company's request, Employee shall return all documents, materials, and copies containing Confidential Information.

---

### 2. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY ASSIGNMENT

**2.1 Work Product.** Employee agrees that all inventions, discoveries, developments, improvements, original works of authorship, trade secrets, and other intellectual property ("Work Product") that Employee conceives, develops, or reduces to practice, alone or with others, during employment, and that:
(a) relate to Company's current or anticipated business, research, or development;
(b) result from any work performed for Company; or
(c) are developed using Company's equipment, supplies, facilities, or Confidential Information;
shall be the sole and exclusive property of Company.

**2.2 Assignment.** Employee hereby irrevocably assigns to Company all right, title, and interest in and to such Work Product, including all intellectual property rights therein.

**2.3 Disclosure.** Employee agrees to promptly disclose to Company all Work Product.

**2.4 Assistance.** Employee agrees to assist Company in obtaining and enforcing patents, copyrights, and other protections for Work Product, at Company's expense.

---

### 3. PRIOR INVENTIONS

**3.1 Excluded Inventions.** Employee has listed on Exhibit A any inventions, discoveries, or improvements that Employee wishes to exclude from this Agreement ("Prior Inventions"). If no list is attached, Employee represents that there are no Prior Inventions.

---

### 4. NON-SOLICITATION

**4.1 Non-Solicitation of Employees.** For a period of twelve (12) months following termination of employment, Employee agrees not to directly or indirectly solicit, recruit, or encourage any employee of Company to leave their employment.

**4.2 Non-Solicitation of Customers.** For a period of twelve (12) months following termination of employment, Employee agrees not to directly or indirectly solicit business from any customer or prospective customer of Company with whom Employee had material contact during employment.

---

### 5. GENERAL PROVISIONS

**5.1 Governing Law.** This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the State of Michigan.

**5.2 Injunctive Relief.** Employee acknowledges that a breach of this Agreement may cause irreparable harm to Company, and Company shall be entitled to seek injunctive relief.

**5.3 Severability.** If any provision is found unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall remain in effect.

**5.4 Entire Agreement.** This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the parties regarding its subject matter.

### Problems

1. **No legal review.** Marcus pulled this template from the internet. A lawyer has never reviewed it. There could be unenforceable provisions.

2. **Michigan law considerations.** Michigan has specific rules about non-competes and non-solicitation. Not sure this complies.

3. **Missing signatures.** We never went back to get existing employees to sign. Marcus said "they already agreed to keep things confidential when they joined." That's not how contracts work.

4. **No tracking.** I don't have a definitive list of who signed and who didn't. Some are in TriNet, some in Google Drive, some in the filing cabinet.

5. **IP assignment scope.** The IP assignment language is very broad. Could be challenged depending on circumstances.

6. **Non-compete clause?** This doesn't have a non-compete but should we have one? Or would it not be enforceable anyway? I don't know Michigan law well enough.

### What Needs to Happen

1. Get legal review of this document
2. Determine if it's enforceable as-is
3. Decide whether to get existing employees to sign
4. Create tracking system for who has/hasn't signed
5. Add to onboarding checklist with verification

### Risk Assessment

**If an employee leaves and takes Confidential Information:**
- For employees who signed: We can pursue legal remedies
- For employees who didn't sign: We have much weaker position

**If an employee claims IP they created here:**
- For employees who signed: Company clearly owns it
- For employees who didn't sign: Potentially murky

**Specific concern:** Several engineers from early days (James Thompson, Kevin Nguyen, Tanya Robinson, etc.) were involved in building the core product and haven't signed IP assignment. If any of them ever claim ownership of their contributions, it could be a major problem for the company and for the Series A due diligence.
