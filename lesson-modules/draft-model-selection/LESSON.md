## The Three Models

You have three Claude models to choose from:

**Claude Sonnet 4.6** (The Default)
- Fast and responsive
- Excellent for most tasks
- Good at coding, analysis, writing, research
- **Use when:** You need balanced speed and intelligence for everyday work

**Claude Opus** (The Power Player)
- Most capable and powerful
- Best for complex reasoning, nuanced analysis, creative thinking
- Takes a bit longer to respond
- **Use when:** You're tackling something really tricky that needs deep thinking — complex strategy, advanced coding, philosophical analysis

**Claude Haiku** (The Speedster)
- Fastest model, great for quick tasks
- Good for straightforward questions and simple jobs
- Lowest latency (barely any wait)
- **Use when:** You just need something quick — a simple lookup, quick summary, fast brainstorm

## When to Switch

Here's a quick guide for when you might want to switch:

**Switch to Opus if:**
- The task feels complex or requires nuanced thinking
- You're stuck and need deeper analysis
- Quality matters more than speed
- You're working on something strategic

**Switch to Haiku if:**
- You just need something quick
- The task is straightforward
- You're testing an idea or getting a fast draft
- You want to save time on simple questions

**Stay with Sonnet if:**
- You're not sure (it's the sweet spot!)
- You're doing general work analysis or writing
- You want good balance of speed and quality

## Important Notes for Claude

- **No technical jargon**: Explain models in terms of speed and usefulness, not training data or parameters
- **Relatable examples**: The real-world task comparisons help more than abstract explanations
- **Empower the choice**: This should feel like "here's a tool you can use" not "here's why one is better than another"
- **Don't oversell switching**: It's useful but not magical — most work happens with Sonnet
- **Make it hands-on**: If the student wants to try switching, guide them through it naturally
