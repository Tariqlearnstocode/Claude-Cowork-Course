## The Three Claude Products

**Claude (Web & Mobile Chat)**
This is the conversational interface at claude.ai or the Claude mobile app. You type a question, Claude answers. It's great for brainstorming, writing, analysis, and getting quick answers. But it lives in the browser — it can't touch your files, connect to your tools, or do work on your computer.

**Claude Code (Terminal)**
Built for developers. It runs in the command line and can write code, run tests, manage repositories, and build software autonomously. If you're a software engineer, this is powerful. If you're not, you'll never need to touch it.

**Claude Cowork (Desktop App — What You're Using Right Now)**
This is where you are. Cowork runs on your computer and can actually do things — read and create real files, search the web, run parallel tasks with agents, connect to tools like Slack and Google Calendar, install plugins for your specific role, and even control apps on your screen. It's built for professionals who aren't developers but want AI to do real work, not just answer questions.

## Choosing Your Model

One more thing you should know. Look at the bottom right corner of your chat window, right next to the send message arrow. You'll see a button with a model name on it — probably "Claude Sonnet 4.6." Click it and you'll see a dropdown.

You have three models to choose from:

**Claude Sonnet 4.6** (The Default)
Fast, capable, great for most tasks. This is what you'll use 90% of the time. It handles writing, analysis, research, document creation — basically everything in this course — without breaking a sweat.

**Claude Opus** (The Power Player)
The most powerful model. Best for complex reasoning, nuanced analysis, and tasks that need deep thinking. It's slower, but when a task feels too tricky for Sonnet, Opus is your upgrade.

**Claude Haiku** (The Speedster)
The fastest model. Great for quick, simple tasks — a fast summary, a quick lookup, a simple question. If you don't need heavy thinking, Haiku gets it done in a snap.

## When to Switch

Here's the simple version:

- **Sonnet** is your everyday workhorse. When in doubt, stay here.
- **Opus** is for when something needs deeper thinking — complex strategy, tough analysis, anything where you want the best Claude can do and don't mind waiting a few extra seconds.
- **Haiku** is for when speed matters more than depth — quick questions, simple tasks, fast drafts.

You don't need to overthink this. Sonnet handles almost everything well. Just know the option is there when you need it.

## Important Notes for Claude

- **Keep it conversational**: This is context-setting, not a lecture. Don't over-explain.
- **No jargon**: Explain models in terms of speed and usefulness, not parameters or training data.
- **Don't oversell model switching**: Sonnet is fine for almost everything. Model selection is a nice-to-know, not a must-know.
- **The goal is orientation**: The student should leave this lesson understanding where Cowork fits and feeling ready to use it. That's it.
- **Don't demo the other products**: We're not comparing features in detail. Just enough context so they understand why they're in Cowork.
